About Bucaan Community House
Who we are

Bucaan Community House Inc. (BCH) is a small not-for-profit charity — DGR endorsed — based in Chigwell, Tasmania, and has been operating for almost 40 years. We are a registered incorporated association governed by a community-led volunteer board of between 4 and 10 members, elected at our Annual General Meeting.

We work from a community development model, serving Austins Ferry, Berridale, Chigwell, Collinsvale, Claremont, Granton, Montrose and Rosetta.

Our vision

To create a safe, supportive, and caring community.

Our mission

To provide accessible local services and support which are valued by our community.

Our governance

BCH is governed by a community-led volunteer board that met 17 times in 2024–2025, including the Chair, Vice Chair, Treasurer, Secretary and general members. Our CEO, Mary-Anne Evans, leads the staff team and is your key contact for any questions about your induction, role or policies.

As a DGR-endorsed registered charity and incorporated association, BCH meets compliance and governance obligations under Tasmanian and Commonwealth law.

Child safe organisation
Core commitment
BCH is a child safe organisation

Keeping vulnerable people and children safe is our top priority. We are committed to creating a safe and caring environment where children are protected from harm. Every child deserves to feel safe, valued, and heard.

📋
WWVP & police check

All staff and volunteers must hold a current WWVP registration AND a current police check before working with children or vulnerable people. Present both to Mary-Anne Evans (CEO) before commencing.

👁️
Appropriate behaviour

Always interact with children in open, observable settings. Never be alone with a child in a closed or private space. Sign the Child Safe Code of Conduct (BCH_CF_30).

🚨
Reporting concerns

Report any concerns immediately to Mary-Anne Evans: (03) 6249 5363. Child Protection Advice and Referral Line: 1800 000 123. Immediate danger: 000.

📱
Online & social media

Do not contact children through personal social media. Use official BCH channels only. Never photograph or film children without explicit written consent from a parent or guardian.

Health, safety & hygiene
🧼
Hand hygiene

Wash and/or sanitise on every entry. Follow the posted procedure in bathrooms and the kitchen. Sanitiser is 70% alcohol-based — let it dry fully.

🧹
Surface sanitising

Sanitise all surfaces you touch — chairs, door handles, light switches, tables, benches, and the urn/tea and coffee area. Disinfectant wipes are provided.

🤧
Cough & sneeze etiquette

Always cough or sneeze into your elbow or a disposable tissue. Dispose of tissues immediately and wash your hands.

🏠
If you are unwell

Stay home if you have any symptoms. Notify Mary-Anne Evans at (03) 6249 5363 as soon as possible.

😷
PPE

Masks and gloves are available near the entry. Take only what you need. Mask requirements will be communicated by Mary-Anne Evans.

🚗
BCH vehicles

All vehicles carry sanitiser, wipes, gloves, rubbish bags and masks. Wipe all touched surfaces before leaving and between pickups. Remove all waste when finished.

During any infectious disease outbreak
Additional measures may be reinstated

BCH may require temperature checks (normal 36.6°C — 37.2°C or above means you must leave), mandatory masks, room capacity limits, and restricted building access. These will be communicated by Mary-Anne Evans.

Emergency evacuation — 8 Bucaan St, Chigwell
In any emergency — call 000 immediately. From a mobile: 112.

Your priority is always the safety of people first. Do not attempt to fight a fire unless it is very small, safe to do so, and you have already raised the alarm.

R
Remove
Remove people from the immediate danger area to a place of safety
A
Alert
Alert all nearby staff, volunteers and visitors — raise the alarm
C
Confine
Close windows and doors if safe to do so to contain fire and smoke
E
Extinguish
Extinguish only if safe — never put yourself at risk
Evacuation stages

Stage 1 — Remove all people from the immediate danger area to safety.

Stage 2 — Complete evacuation of the entire building.

Stage 3 — Roll call. Ensure all occupants are accounted for. Report missing persons to the Tasmanian Fire Service.

Stage 4 — Move to your designated assembly area.

Assembly areas
🟢
General assembly area
Exit via the front door facing the car park — move to the steps in front of the Barn.
🟠
Fire in the offices
Exit via the safest exit to the front of the building — proceed to the Gazebo facing Bucaan Street.
🔵
Fire in the kitchen
Exit via the front door facing the car park — move to the steps in front of the Barn.
